# Yulendj Barring for Sonic Practice (sketch)

# Introduction

At the conclusion of the 2026 Australian Posthuman Summer Lab (APSL), I spoke with Associate Prof. Troy Innocent regarding the prospect of using the Yulendj Barring framework in my PhD project.  This document is to sketch my initial thoughts regarding how the core concepts of the Yulendj Barring framework map to my existing ontological and methodological frameworks. 

This move responds to feedback from my last milestone regarding ways I might better acknowledge the current (post-)colonial historical situation of my research project, and ground my practice more meaningfully here on the country of the Boonwurrung and Wurundjeri people.

# Knowledge Paths

The three iterations of APSL (2024, 2025, and 2026) represent a collaborative project between Professor N'arwee't Carolyn Briggs AM, Professor Rosi Braidotti, and the Planetary Civics Inquiry here at RMIT, to embark on a practical mapping of the conceptual territory between continental philosophy and Indigenous knowledges.  As part of these labs, participants attended lectures, workshops, and collaborated in small groups with a view to exploring, mapping, and applying an Indigenous framework introduced to us as the *Yulendj Barring*, which translates from N'arwee't Carolyn Briggs' native Boonwurrung language as *Knowledge Paths*.

As part of the small group collaborations, I took the opportunity to road-test with my group a potential mapping for Yulendj Barring's four constituent concepts (*Kummargii*, *Dhumbali*, *Djilbruk*, and *Tjanabi*), informed by the framework I have been constructing for the purposes of my research on distributed synthesis, as well as my recent reading on the philosophy of Spinoza and Braidotti's own critical posthumanism.

What follows is a rough attempt to explain my intuitions about how each of these four concepts could potentially be mapped for the purposes of my research project, and sonic practice more generally, with notes on practical application drawn from my experiences at APSL 2026.

# Kummargii

Introduced as a rough analog for Braidotti's *zoe*, or *regeneration*, as I understand it, *Kummargii* denotes Country, in its vital materiality.  

The way I would like to attempt to explain this is to render it from multiple angles more or less simultaneously.  For example, we could start with Kant's *noumena* -- that speculative dimension of materiality we suspect to exist, which structures and underwrites perceivable phenomena.  However, this by itself would not do justice to *Kummargii*'s vital capacity, and here my intuition is to reach for distinction Hume draws in his famous *is-ought gap*:  for Hume, prescriptive conclusions regarding what one *ought* to do, can never be deduced from descriptions and logic alone -- a logically sound deduction of a prescriptive axiom relies on the presence of at least one prescriptive axiom.  In many ways, the history of ethical inquiry within the philosophy of British empiricism related to the search for a way to get to *prescriptions* (ie. *ought*), from *descriptions* (ie. *is*).

The error born out in this history is exactly the Braidottian conceit regarding the Man of Logos, ie. the problem lies in its construction of the human as beginning and belonging on the side of *descriptions*.  *Noumena*, within this (hegemonic) schematisation, would lie on the *descriptive* side, and the job for human endeavour is to then find a way to "jump" from the descriptive, to the *prescriptive*.

The key to understanding Kummargii then is to understand that *noumena* was, and is, *prescriptive* to begin with, and what we understand to be the *descriptive*, ie. *language*, Logos, or Lacan's *Symbolic*, to be contingent at best, or a facade, mirage, or worse.  The representational account of language, which the empiricists took to be axiomatic, creates the illusion that this bubble of descriptions we live our lives in, to be the default resting state of the universe outside our purview.  *Kummargii* then denotes a *prescriptive* noumenon - that pre-lingual ocean of implicit prescriptive conceptual content that lies just beyond phenomena, capable of structuring the whole material reality of Country, and of giving rise to subjectivity and consciousness.

Kummargii as such does not distinguish between nature and culture, and it is with this in mind that I consider the words "prescriptive" and "normative" to be synonymous in this context.

Similarly, I believe there to be utility in mapping Kummargii to Lacan's *Real* also, although the nuances of such a mapping will need to remain outside the scope of this document for the time being.  I will note, however, that Braidotti's critical posthumanism finds strange bedfellows with Lacan's structural psychoanalysis, largely, I believe, due to their common ancestor in Spinoza.  A full exposition of this connection would be a lofty but wholly worthwhile undertaking for someone, somewhere, at some point, in my opinion.  Prof. Braidotti sketched a possible approach, and suggested to me some preliminary readings for this purpose at the concluding drinks of APSL 2026.

# Dhumbali

*Dhumbali* was introduced as *commitment*, or *promise*.  My ears pricked up at this, as, along inferentialist lines, when I hear "commitment", my mind raises to question of *subjectivity*.  The reason for this is related to the schema drawn up for Kummargii in the previous section.

According to Brandom, Kant's most important insight, was that we, as subjects, are constituted by our commitments.  For Brandom, these constitutive commitments are exactly the set of commitments required for language use.  We might pause for a moment here to appreciate the clarity this vocabulary affords us in relation to the descriptive / normative dichotomy we have been employing thus far.  In order to inhabit a world of descriptions, one must be *already committed* to the set of rules which govern the language used to make those descriptions.  Our bodies, as the Lacanians would have it, are *ensnared* by the normativity of language.

It is worth explicating here that these commitments are not similar to identifications regarding football team you barrack for, or which political party you vote for, or your status as a socialist, feminist, nihilist, etc.  The commitments here are largely unconscious, giving rise to what we could term the "modern moment" - a point in discourse at which one must inflect to make an explicit account of what one has hitherto been implicitly committed to.

This entry into language is an important pivoting point for a couple of reasons. First, we might appreciate the role sound plays in facilitating this entry into language: what an infant hears listening to the language use of adults are exactly the constituent elements of music -- pitch, timbre, amplitude, melody, phrasing, dynamics, tempo, etc.  Over time, using *identification* and *inference*, the toddler acquires those commitments required for language use.  Second, I hope it is legible for us to understand that via the action of these musical elements, the normativity of Kummargii to enter a human body to constitute *subjectivity*.

This by itself does not define *Dhumbali* -- I would like to attach a disclaimer, which I feel like I can express most clearly in Lacanian terms.  Subjectivity which forecloses its castration in relation to Kummargii, and which is formally psychotic in structure, ceases to be Dhumbali.  My intuition is that Dhumbali can only exist within a humbled relation to Nature where castration has been accepted, and it is this distinction which characterises non-fascist positive projects from fascist ones.

# Djilbruk

Mycelial creativity!  Gradual, accumulative, omnidirectional, transformational outgrowth.

# Tjanabi

The fruiting body!  An eruption of vitality, spreading spores.
